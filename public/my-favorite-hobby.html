My Story

I can clearly remember when I was around 9 years old, my mother would let me borrow her iPod, and I would mostly listen to songs that I knew from the radio, and that was a pretty normal thing to do, but one day, when my mom got new music, I opened the door into my long journy into metal music. My mother got Metallica's S&M album, and the first song I heard from it was Battery, and it was the heaviest thing I could imagine. I listend to it nonstop, and fixated on Metallica for about 4 years. But as I got older, I started to want something new, I journied into Power Metal with Man'O'War and Dragonforce. But after a phase of power metal, I wanted something heavier, so I moved into melodic death metal with Amon Amarth. When you start listening to death metal, and you like it, you start to crave heavier and HEAVIER metal. That is the phase I would say I am in right now, I have not lost love for any of the lighter metal, but I have gained a interest in finding nastier and heavier metal.

Over the years of listening to metal, one of my favorite things to do, involving metal, is going to concerts. I can't even begin to think of how many concerts I have gone to, some of them blur together, but I have so many great memories from concerts. I get to enjoy the music I love, be with friends, have a good time, and mosh and headbang till I know I will be sore the next few days. By going to concerts I have also been able to see musicians that I idolize. I have been fortunate enough to see such legends as Iron Maidan, Slayer, Megadeth, and not only Ozzy on his own, TWICE, but I even was lucky enough to go to Black Sabbath's final tour! Being able to see the band that was resposible for creating the genre 50 YEARS AGO still blows my mind.
